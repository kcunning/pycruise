Costs

There are a number of options when it comes to rooms, so make sure to read this section carefully. If you just want the bottom line, head to the bottom of the page.

Room types

The first thing you should consider is what kind of room you want. We have three types of rooms available on PyCruise:

Interior. Interior rooms are on the interior of thie ship, and are the most economic option. They have no windows, so are perfect for someone who wants to save some money, or someone who doesn't want to be woken up at sunrise (sun comes up early at sea).

Oceanview. Ocean view rooms offer a view of the sea. These are a great option for anyone who wants natural light, but don't want a balcony (this is a great option for parents of overly inventive children).

Balcony. Balcony rooms come with a private balcony and are usually somewhat larger than interior rooms. Balconies are perfect for people who want a private space to escape to, so that they can enjoy the sea or views of the ports.

Gratuity

There is the option to pre-pay for gratuity, which I strongly recommend doing. This is around $15 a day (though this can vary slightly). Gratuity covers the wait staff, your steward, and the cleaning staff. Gratuity does not change based on what room you pick.

Taxes and fees

Each port has its own taxes and fees. For this trip, the taxes and fees are $165 a person. This does not change based on what room you pick. These fees are not included in the room prices below.

Occupancy

All of the room prices are based on double occupancy. If you want a room to yourself, your room price is going to be a bit more (the contract doesn't have the exact amount, so I'll track that down).

If you have more than two people, you will be limited in what rooms you can reserve, since not every room can accomodate an extra bed (and yes, everyone needs to have a bed!). There are, however, many rooms that come with a fold-out couch, or that can fit another rolling bed. Just let the Carnival agent know, so they can pick out the right room for you.

In general, if you have more than two people, the cost for the extra people goes down slightly.

Room rates

We have a variety of rooms available, each with their own price range (rooms on certain parts of the ship cost a bit more).
